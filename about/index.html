A bit about me

I'm a data scientist who somehow ended up dipping my toes in everything from survey engineering to AI chatbot building to bank churn models — and through all of it, kept gravitating back to the part many people gloss over: making the data actually usable. I've worked across health, finance, agriculture, trade, and telecoms, building dashboards, predictive models, and NLP pipelines for organizations tackling real problems. I also teach, train, and occasionally find myself on a conference stage talking about either chatbots or being a woman in this field (sometimes both).

These days, I'm pivoting toward analytics engineering. I love machine learning,especially NLP, I'm comfortable doing research, and I can build a churn model or a topic-modelling pipeline without blinking — but if I'm honest, what I actually enjoy most is the plumbing: clean data warehouses, solid pipelines, and dashboards that don't break the moment someone refreshes them. So while I'm open to roles in data engineering, analytics, NLP/LLMs, ML, and applied research, analytics engineering is where I'd choose to live if given the option.
